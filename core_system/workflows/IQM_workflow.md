# IQ Mindware Power-Led Business Workflow

## Strategic purpose

The workflow should ensure that every week develops several forms of business power:

| Power function                     | Business expression                                                          |
| ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Protect F★**                     | Preserve energy, cash, attention and scientific reputation                   |
| **Expand 𝒢-volume**               | Open new markets, partnerships, products and distribution routes             |
| **Improve λ**                      | Maintain contact with users, partners, analytics and market reality          |
| **Convert Gf → Gc**                | Turn ideas and research into protocols, products, articles and systems       |
| **Control the meaning niche**      | Define Cognitive Performance Infrastructure, H-AGI and far transfer          |
| **Build truth-coupled reputation** | Publish evidence, claims boundaries, methods and honest limitations          |
| **Bank institutional power**       | Build owned media, datasets, CRM systems, protocols and repeatable processes |
| **Deploy power**                   | Make explicit offers, partner requests, sales asks and pilot proposals       |

The central operating rule is:

> **Every week should build perception, proof, product, partnership, pipeline and reusable structure.**

Content is one component of this system—not the whole system.

---

# 1. The media architecture

Do not create separate ideas for every platform. Develop one core intellectual or commercial object and distribute it across several surfaces.

```text
Scientific or market insight
→ canonical website asset
→ Substack article or Strategy Lab protocol
→ LinkedIn posts
→ Substack Notes
→ YouTube demonstration
→ Brevo email
→ partner one-pager
→ product or protocol improvement
```

## Role of each medium

| Medium                        | Strategic role                                                               |                        Recommended rhythm |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------: |
| **IQMindware.com**            | Owned commercial hub, SEO, product pages, proof and canonical knowledge base | One new page or substantial update weekly |
| **LinkedIn**                  | Category framing, professional discovery and partner relationships           |                        Three posts weekly |
| **Substack Notes**            | Frequent idea testing, audience contact and distribution                     |                      Three to five weekly |
| **Free Substack articles**    | Trust, education and demand generation                                       |                               Two monthly |
| **Paid H-AGI / Strategy Lab** | Premium protocols, tools, worked examples and GPT-linked systems             |                               Two monthly |
| **YouTube**                   | Demonstration, evergreen discovery and protocol explanation                  |            Two substantial videos monthly |
| **Short video clips**         | Extend the life of full videos                                               |                      Three to six monthly |
| **Brevo**                     | Owned distribution, conversion, reactivation and product onboarding          |                          One email weekly |
| **Email/Discord support**     | Customer intelligence, retention and product feedback                        |              Two time-boxed checks weekly |
| **Product/app development**   | Delivery, behavioural data and commercial value                              |           One material improvement weekly |
| **Partner outreach**          | B2B pilots, trusted distribution and niche entry                             |       Five to ten targeted actions weekly |
| **Research/proof materials**  | Scientific credibility and defensibility                                     |       One meaningful proof update monthly |

The website should become the canonical home for durable, search-relevant material. Substack and LinkedIn should distribute, interpret and humanise those ideas. Time-sensitive opinions do not all need to become website pages, but enduring explanatory, scientific, protocol and “wiki” material generally should.

# 2. Weekly operating rhythm

## Monday — Observe and frame

### Purpose

Maintain contact with reality and determine what deserves attention.

### Actions

* Review website, LinkedIn, Substack, Brevo and product signals.
* Review customer questions, comments, partner conversations and support issues.
* Examine relevant scientific, AI-workplace, education and performance developments.
* Select one audience for the week.
* Select one **demand thesis**.
* Select one primary offer and CTA.
* Decide which owned asset will be built.

### Example demand theses

* AI makes answers cheaper, but judgement scarcer.
* Cognitive offloading creates a new need for evidence control.
* Training-task improvement is not evidence of far transfer.
* Cognitive readiness should be measured before demanding performance.
* Human cognitive capacity is becoming the control layer beneath AI work.

### Outputs

* One weekly thesis.
* One audience.
* One offer.
* One asset brief.
* One LinkedIn framing post.
* One Substack Note.

---

## Tuesday — Build and prove

### Purpose

Convert scientific and commercial thinking into owned intellectual property.

### Choose one principal task

* Improve a protocol.
* Add instrumentation or scoring logic.
* Develop a transfer probe.
* Improve an app workflow.
* Create a proof or score-explanation page.
* Clarify a claims boundary.
* Improve onboarding or activation.
* Define a B2B pilot component.
* Produce a scientific evidence note.
* Create a product walkthrough.
* Improve a control-surface mock-up.

### Outputs

* One banked product, protocol or proof asset.
* Updated protocol/version documentation where relevant.
* One short fragment explaining what changed and why.

This is the most important protection against becoming merely a content business. The underlying strategic value lies in the protocol engine, workflow applications, measurement architecture and organisational control surfaces.

---

## Wednesday — Publish and establish authority

### Purpose

Control the meaning niche and make the week’s intellectual asset discoverable.

### Week A

Publish a substantial asset:

* website article or wiki page;
* free Substack article;
* paid H-AGI Strategy Lab protocol;
* research or evidence article;
* major protocol explanation.

### Week B

Publish an applied asset:

* case study;
* worked example;
* implementation guide;
* protocol variation;
* FAQ;
* objection response;
* claims-boundary article;
* “how this works in practice” post.

### Distribution

* Publish one LinkedIn post.
* Publish one or two Substack Notes.
* Add relevant internal website links.
* Link the article to a product, mailing list, demonstration or pilot route.

The aim is not simply to explain science. It is to establish concepts that the market increasingly associates with IQ Mindware:

```text
Cognitive Performance Infrastructure
scientifically hardened cognitive protocols
human cognitive-capacity layer
horizontal and vertical far transfer
AI Workflow Cognition
Measure → Train → Regulate → Prove → Deploy
```

---

## Thursday — Demonstrate and build alliances

### Purpose

Turn intellectual authority into trust, relationships and deployment opportunities.

### Week A: Demonstration

* Record or publish one YouTube demonstration.
* Show a protocol, GPT, app, assessment or workflow in operation.
* Extract three potential short clips.
* Embed the video on a relevant website page.
* Send it directly to selected partners or prospects.

### Week B: Partnership and application

* Publish clips from the Week A video.
* Send five to ten carefully targeted partner messages.
* Follow up with previous contacts.
* Improve a pilot proposal or partner one-pager.
* Hold or request one relevant conversation.
* Ask for a test, introduction, pilot discussion or expert review.

Priority partner groups include:

* tutors and exam-preparation organisations;
* executive and performance coaches;
* AI-workflow consultants;
* L&D and workplace-wellbeing professionals;
* universities and research collaborators;
* health, resilience and performance practitioners;
* digital platforms that could embed a protocol.

Alliance activity should produce more than goodwill. It should bank a shared asset, feedback route, introduction, dataset, pilot, distribution channel or repeatable process.

---

## Friday — Convert and systemise

### Purpose

Turn attention and trust into revenue, activation and organisational capacity.

### Commercial actions

* Send one Brevo email.
* Use one primary CTA.
* Follow up with warm leads.
* Check checkout and onboarding.
* Improve one offer or landing page.
* Invite readers to a product, paid Substack, pilot, waitlist or call.

### Systems actions

* Update CRM records.
* Record objections and partner needs.
* Add internal website links.
* Progress one SEO migration or page refresh.
* Improve an onboarding email.
* Update FAQ or proof material.
* Complete the second weekly support/community check.

Potential weekly Brevo CTAs include:

```text
Try a cognitive test
Read the full protocol
Subscribe to H-AGI
Join the IQ Coach waitlist
Purchase IQ Pro
View a demonstration
Discuss a pilot
Reply with a current cognitive bottleneck
```

Avoid presenting every offer in one email. Each week should have one principal commercial path.

---

## Saturday — Bank and re-coordinate

### Purpose

Turn activity into reusable learning rather than beginning again from zero.

### Weekly review

Record:

* best-performing idea;
* strongest search or engagement signal;
* most important customer question;
* qualified leads and partner replies;
* product activation or sales;
* scientific or claims issue discovered;
* asset completed;
* system improved;
* work that should be repeated;
* work that should be stopped;
* next likely demand thesis.

### Repurposing

Use Saturday to prepare:

* one founder or weekly recap;
* remaining Substack Notes;
* LinkedIn fragments;
* video clips;
* internal links;
* quotations or graphics for later use.

### Power audit

Ask:

```text
Did the week protect my operating base?
Did it expand viable options?
What contact with reality changed my model?
What permanent asset was banked?
What explicit commercial action was taken?
Did I strengthen or weaken dependence on external platforms?
```

---

## Sunday — Protect the base

No mandatory business output.

Reading, scientific synthesis and creative exploration are appropriate only when restorative. The workflow should not obtain visible growth by consuming the cognitive base required to sustain the company.

# 3. The fortnightly campaign cycle

The most sustainable structure is a two-week campaign around one demand thesis.

## Week A — Create and launch

```text
Identify pressure
→ formulate thesis
→ build protocol/product/proof asset
→ publish substantial article
→ publish Strategy Lab tool
→ record demonstration
→ make initial offer
```

Typical outputs:

* one canonical website page;
* one free or paid Substack article;
* one video;
* two or three LinkedIn posts;
* several Notes;
* one Brevo email;
* one product or proof improvement.

## Week B — Apply and convert

```text
Observe response
→ publish worked example
→ distribute clips
→ answer objections
→ approach partners
→ strengthen CTA
→ improve onboarding
→ bank lessons
```

Typical outputs:

* one case study or application article;
* three video clips;
* partner outreach;
* one email;
* one SEO or conversion improvement;
* one CRM follow-up round;
* one claims or evidence refinement.

This allows each important idea to become a small campaign rather than a disposable social-media post.

# 4. Monthly operating cycle

The weekly workflow continues throughout the month, but each week has a dominant strategic emphasis.

## Week 1 — Explore the niche and set the agenda

Decide:

* primary market;
* audience;
* demand thesis;
* offer;
* language to test;
* proof gap;
* substantial article;
* commercial target.

The growth-lever hierarchy suggests beginning with the scaling hypothesis, niche fit and claims boundaries—not with automation or maximum content volume.

## Week 2 — Build protocol, product and proof

Complete:

* one meaningful protocol or product improvement;
* one measurement or semantic-spine improvement;
* one onboarding improvement;
* one claims review;
* one proof or evidence asset.

## Week 3 — Deploy through partners and pilots

Complete:

* ten to twenty targeted outreach actions;
* two to five explicit conversation requests;
* one pilot or partner-facing document;
* one demonstration;
* one commercial proposal improvement;
* one partner feedback review.

## Week 4 — Bank, consolidate and reallocate

Review:

* revenue and conversion;
* product activation and retention;
* email performance;
* website and SEO performance;
* partner and pilot pipeline;
* proof and validation progress;
* customer questions;
* operating costs;
* time and energy expenditure.

Then decide:

```text
repeat
expand
modify
merge
redirect
pause
stop
automate
```

Automation belongs here only after the underlying workflow has demonstrated value and stability.

# 8. Operating guardrails

## 1. One core idea, multiple surfaces

Do not write a separate original piece for every medium.

## 2. One primary audience and offer per month

Other offers may remain available, but the campaign should have a clear centre.

## 3. Every week must create an owned asset

Posts alone do not sufficiently bank business power.

## 4. Every week must include an explicit ask

Publishing without deployment builds recognition but not necessarily a business.

## 5. Proof precedes aggressive scaling

Claims boundaries, validation logic, measurement quality and onboarding should mature alongside growth.

## 6. Owned channels reduce capture

The website, mailing lists, product data, protocols and partner relationships should become progressively more valuable than dependence on any individual social platform.

## 7. Automation comes last

Automate a working loop, not an unclear or ineffective one.

## 8. The schedule protects the operator

A task that repeatedly overruns should be simplified, batched, delegated, reduced in frequency or stopped.

```text
MONDAY

Build and prove

WEDNESDAY
Publish and establish authority

THURSDAY
Demonstrate and build alliances

FRIDAY
Convert and systemise

SATURDAY
Bank and re-coordinate

```

Across a fortnight:

```text
Week A: create and launch
Week B: apply and convert
```

Across a month:

```text
Explore the niche
→ build the protocol and proof
→ deploy through partners
→ bank, consolidate and reallocate
```

The decisive question is not:

> How much content did I publish?

It is:

> **Did this month leave IQ Mindware with stronger protocols, better evidence, more owned distribution, deeper relationships, clearer market positioning, greater revenue potential and more viable paths than it had at the beginning?**
