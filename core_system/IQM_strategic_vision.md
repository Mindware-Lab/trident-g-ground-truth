# IQ Mindware Strategic Architecture

## Cognitive workflow apps and human-capacity control surfaces

## 1. Market shift

The market is moving from:

> apps that help people practise general cognitive skills

to:

> systems that help people measure, train, regulate and protect cognitive capacity inside real workflows.

This is the core strategic shift for IQ Mindware.

The opportunity is not simply to sell “brain training”. The stronger opportunity is to build **Cognitive Performance Infrastructure**: a practical system for helping individuals, students, professionals and organisations understand cognitive state, train key capacities and support better decisions under pressure.

The wider technology trend is the move from fragmented tools to **control surfaces**: integrated operational dashboards that turn separate signals into situation awareness, bottleneck detection, prediction, decision support and action. The same logic applies to cognition. Cognitive performance should not sit outside the workflow as an isolated app. It should become part of the workflow itself.

In simple terms:

> **The apps help people manage attention, reasoning and overload.**
> **The dashboards help managers support teams, students or clients more intelligently.**

This gives IQ Mindware a dual role:

1. **Workflow apps for individuals**
2. **Cognitive control surfaces for organisations**

## 2. Strategic category

**Cognitive Performance Infrastructure**

Not:

* brain training

## 3. Core strategic pitch

AI, digital overload and high-pressure work are increasing the speed, complexity and fragmentation of daily cognition.

The bottleneck is still human:

* attention
* working memory
* reasoning
* judgement
* cognitive resilience
* state regulation
* source and context tracking
* decision readiness

IQ Mindware becomes the **human capacity layer beneath AI-era work, study and performance**.

The platform answers two sets of questions.

### For individuals

* What is my current cognitive state?
* Am I ready to focus, train, decide or recover?
* Am I overloaded, under-activated, narrow or scattered?
* What should I do next: train, reset, widen, activate, rest or slow-check?
* Am I improving over time?

### For managers, coaches and organisations

* Are people engaging with the cognitive support system?
* Are readiness or overload patterns emerging?
* Which groups may need more support?
* Which cognitive bottlenecks are appearing: attention, memory, reasoning, overload or transfer?
* Are interventions producing credible progress signals?
* Where should support, training or workflow redesign be targeted?

This makes IQ Mindware both a **personal cognitive workflow layer** and a **managerial support surface**.

## 4. The dual product architecture

## A. Workflow apps for individuals

These are short, guided tools that sit inside daily work, study, coaching or wellbeing routines.

They help people:

* check readiness
* train attention
* practise working memory and reasoning
* recover from overload
* manage cognitive pressure
* avoid pushing through when the state is poor
* build a personal cognitive baseline over time

Example user loop:

```text
Start work / study / training block
→ 3-minute readiness or attention check
→ plain-language state result
→ recommended next step
→ train / reset / widen / activate / rest
→ completion card
→ progress update
```

This matches the existing UX principle that the product should feel like a guided daily route, not a confusing dashboard-first experience. The current strategic document already describes the desired loop as: baseline → state check → targeted training → bottleneck detection → transfer check → applied deployment → delayed follow-up → updated recommendation. 

## B. Control surfaces for managers and organisations

These are dashboards or reports that sit above individual app use.

They help organisations see patterns without turning the product into surveillance.

They can show:

* aggregate engagement
* readiness trends
* cognitive load patterns
* common recommended routes
* attention or reasoning bottlenecks
* group progress over time
* cohort-level training uptake
* whether transfer or delayed re-checks are improving
* where support may be needed

Example manager loop:

```text
View cohort / team dashboard
→ identify readiness or overload pattern
→ see recommended support route
→ adjust workflow, training or support
→ review progress after intervention
→ update pilot or programme design
```

The control surface should be framed as:

> **A support dashboard, not a surveillance system.**

It should not show diagnostic labels, mental-health risk scores or punitive individual rankings.

## 6. Core platform logic

The platform is built around five functions:

| Function     | Individual app role                                                      | Manager control-surface role                                          |
| ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Measure**  | Helps the person check state and capacity.                               | Shows group-level readiness and capacity trends.                      |
| **Train**    | Gives targeted cognitive practice.                                       | Shows training uptake and progress signals.                           |
| **Regulate** | Routes the person to train, reset, widen, activate or rest.              | Shows where overload, under-activation or narrow focus may be common. |
| **Prove**    | Builds personal baselines and progress records.                          | Builds aggregate pilot evidence and confidence labels.                |
| **Deploy**   | Connects cognitive skills to work, study, wellness or decision contexts. | Shows whether support is working inside real workflows.               |

So the strategic stack becomes:

```text
Measure → Train → Regulate → Prove → Deploy
```

but now expressed as:

```text
Workflow apps → individual support
Control surfaces → organisational intelligence
```

## 7. Scientific engine

The scientific engine remains Trident-G and the IQ Mindware cognitive stack.

The platform should not reward local game improvement alone. It should test whether cognitive operations survive:

* changed formats
* lures
* delay
* reasoning transfer
* applied use

The existing document already states that the Trident-G protocol does not reward local game improvement alone, but tests whether a relation survives wrapper change, near-miss lures, mixed presentation, vertical reconstruction, applied use and delay. 

That matters commercially because it separates IQ Mindware from ordinary brain-training apps.

The product claim should be:

> **We train and track whether cognitive skills remain useful when the task, pressure or context changes.**

Not:

> “This raises IQ.”
> “This diagnoses attention.”
> “This proves clinical improvement.”

## 10. Manager control-surface principles

The control surface should follow five principles.

### 1. Aggregate first

Show team, cohort or client-group trends before individual detail.

### 2. Support, not surveillance

The dashboard should help managers improve conditions and support people, not rank or punish them.

### 3. No clinical labels

Avoid:

* anxiety score
* burnout diagnosis
* cognitive deficit
* brain weakness
* mental-health risk

Use:

* readiness trend
* training engagement
* possible overload pattern
* support route
* current training focus

### 4. Show confidence

Every score or trend should have a confidence label:

```text
insufficient data
calibrating
moderate confidence
high confidence
timing limited
unstable estimate
```

### 5. Connect insight to action

The dashboard should never just display data. It should recommend support routes:

```text
increase recovery windows
offer reset route
reduce novelty stacking
schedule reasoning check
use lower-load training route
review after 7 days
```

This is what makes it a control surface rather than a report.

## 11. Ethical positioning

The control-surface trend has a risk: dashboards can become capture surfaces.

A cognitive dashboard could easily become a system for ranking, pressuring or monitoring people. IQ Mindware should explicitly take the opposite position.

Ethical differentiator:

> **IQ Mindware protects the human inside the workflow.**

The goal is not to downgrade the person into a data point. The goal is to help people remain attentive, resilient, reflective and capable of judgement under pressure.

Manager-facing wording:

> We provide privacy-respecting group insights to help organisations support cognitive readiness and resilience. We do not diagnose individuals, replace professional judgement or provide punitive monitoring.

The document already emphasises that the proof layer is central to trust, research credibility, partner confidence, defensible claims, normative database growth and B2B reporting.

| **Manager / organisation** | Is this programme useful? Where is support needed? Is there evidence to continue or scale? |
